## Our Mission

**Elevate the appraisal industry by transforming transactional vendor-bank relationships into professional partnerships built on respect, transparency, and mutual success.**

---

## The Problem We Solve

### For Vendors (Appraisers)

**Primary Pain Point:**
> "If you work for 10 of our banks, you'd have to update these same exact credentials 10 different times for 10 different banks." - Sunda Scanlon

**The Credential Hell:**
- Vendors serve multiple banks (often 8-10+)
- Each bank requires separate credential updates
- Same licenses, insurance, W-9 uploaded repeatedly
- No synchronization or centralization
- Massive time waste for professionals

**The Relationship Problem:**
> "I'll send in my work. I don't know how I'm doing... I don't get any kudos... The only time I hear back is when they have a problem." - Jeff Hicks

**The Feedback Vacuum:**
- Submit 10 appraisals → hear nothing
- Only contacted when problems arise
- Never acknowledged for good work
- Purely criticism-based relationship
- Feel undervalued despite professionalism

**The Identity Problem:**
> "Even calling somebody a vendor is lightly offensive. It's not a relationship, right?" - Jeff Hicks

**The Professional Disrespect:**
- Licensed professionals treated as commodities
- Term "vendor" feels derogatory
- Want to be viewed as professional partners
- No mechanism for industry elevation
- Stuck in transactional, arm's-length dynamic

### For Banks (Lenders)

**Vendor Management Overhead:**
- Managing hundreds of vendor relationships
- Credential verification burden
- Compliance and audit requirements
- Quality control challenges
- Communication inefficiencies

**Risk Management:**
- Need current, accurate vendor credentials
- Must ensure license validity
- Insurance coverage verification
- Compliance with FIRREA and Fair Lending
- Audit trail requirements

**Relationship Challenges:**
- 50% of appraisals require revisions (industry norm)
- Lack of constructive communication channels
- No feedback mechanism for vendors
- Transactional relationship limits quality improvement
- Can't elevate vendor performance effectively

---

## Our Solution

### Phase 1: The Central Profile (Q1 2026)

**One Profile. All Banks. Finally.**

**Core Innovation:**
- Single vendor profile on central server
- Update credentials once → propagates to all connected banks
- Each bank reviews and accepts changes independently
- Vendor privacy maintained (banks don't see each other)
- Modern, mobile-native interface
- Intuitive, respectful design

**Key Features:**
- My Requests (unified view across all banks)
- My Invites (bank connection requests)
- My Documents (credentials, licenses, insurance)
- Profile Management (coverage, specialties, professional info)
- Multi-user business support (appraisal offices with multiple appraisers)

**Value Proposition:**
- **For Vendors:** Update once, not 10 times. Save hours. Work anywhere.
- **For Banks:** Always-current credentials. Automatic notifications. Compliance simplified.

### Phase 2: The Relationship Platform (Future)

**From Transactions to Relationships**

> "Improving that relationship would be fantastic from both sides of the fence. And it's never really been viewed that way. It's never been tried." - Jeff Hicks

**Relationship Features:**
- Feedback and recognition system
- Kudos for good work
- Relationship health indicators
- Constructive suggestions (non-order-specific)
- Professional community (subtle, LinkedIn-like)
- Industry elevation tools

**Within Regulatory Constraints:**
> "We can't have communication about a report in here, it's got to stay on the order because audit purposes requires that all information stays there." - Sunda Scanlon

- NO order-specific discussions
- General relationship feedback only
- Performance indicators and trends
- Professional development suggestions
- Community engagement (non-order-specific)

**Value Proposition:**
- **For Vendors:** Feel valued. Get feedback. Professional recognition. Career growth.
- **For Banks:** Better vendor relationships → better vendor performance → better outcomes.

**This doesn't have to be the way.**

**What if:**

**This is what we're building toward.**

**We're not just building software. We're elevating an industry.**

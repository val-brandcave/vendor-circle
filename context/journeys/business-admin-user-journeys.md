## Current Pain Points (Before Vendors Circle)

### 1. Credential Management Nightmare
**Problem:** Each appraiser updates credentials separately for each bank
- 5 appraisers × 12 banks = 60 separate profile updates
- Sarah spends 10+ hours/month tracking license expirations
- Staff forgets to update, banks complain
- No centralized view of team credentials

**Impact:** High administrative burden, compliance risk

### 2. No Team-Level View
**Problem:** Current system treats each appraiser as independent
- Can't see aggregate team coverage
- No way to assign work based on availability
- Can't track team performance
- Each appraiser manages own bank relationships (fragmented)

**Impact:** Inefficiency, missed opportunities, poor resource allocation

### 3. Individual-Centric Software
**Problem:** Existing platforms designed for solo appraisers
- No business entity concept
- No user management
- No team collaboration features
- Sarah has to log in as each appraiser to help them

**Impact:** Doesn't match business reality, creates workarounds

### 4. Bank Communication Fragmented
**Problem:** Each appraiser communicates with banks independently
- Sarah doesn't know who's talking to which bank
- Can't maintain consistent business relationship
- Missed opportunities for preferential treatment
- Banks deal with 5 different people (no single contact)

**Impact:** Weaker bank relationships, inconsistent communication

### 5. Work Assignment Manual
**Problem:** Banks send work to individual appraisers
- No way to reassign if someone's overloaded
- Can't balance workload across team
- Junior appraisers underutilized
- Senior appraisers overwhelmed

**Impact:** Burnout, inefficiency, revenue loss

## Key Workflows

### Workflow 1: Invite New Team Member

**Frequency:** Monthly (as business grows)  
**Time:** 30 minutes  
**Steps:**
1. Click "Invite Team Member"
2. Enter email and role
3. Send invitation
4. Team member receives email
5. Team member creates account
6. Team member completes profile
7. Admin reviews and approves
8. Admin links user to profile (if appraiser)
9. Team member can start working

**Success Criteria:**
- Invitation sent within 2 minutes
- Team member can complete setup independently
- Admin only needs to review/approve

---

### Workflow 2: Create Appraiser Profile

**Frequency:** As needed (new hires, profile separation)  
**Time:** 15 minutes  
**Steps:**
1. Go to Appraiser Profiles page
2. Click "Create New Profile"
3. Enter appraiser info:
   - Name
   - Specialties
   - Coverage areas
4. Add licenses:
   - State
   - License number
   - Expiration
   - Upload document
5. Add credentials:
   - W-9
   - Resume
   - Sample reports
   - Insurance
6. Review and save
7. Assign to user (optional)

**Success Criteria:**
- Profile created in < 20 minutes
- All required fields captured
- Can be assigned to user immediately or later

---

### Workflow 3: Assign Work to Team Member

**Frequency:** Daily  
**Time:** 5 minutes per assignment  
**Steps:**
1. Go to Team's Requests
2. View unassigned work or existing assignments
3. Select order(s)
4. Click "Assign to..."
5. Select team member from dropdown
6. Add note (optional)
7. Confirm assignment
8. Team member gets notification
9. Order appears in their My Requests

**Success Criteria:**
- Can assign in < 1 minute
- Team member notified immediately
- Clear tracking of who's working on what

---

### Workflow 4: Monitor Team Performance

**Frequency:** Weekly  
**Time:** 10 minutes  
**Steps:**
1. Open Business Overview Dashboard
2. Review key metrics:
   - Active orders
   - Team utilization
   - Turnaround times
   - Revision rates
3. Check individual performance (Team Members list)
4. Identify issues (overload, underutilization, quality concerns)
5. Take action (reassign work, provide training, celebrate success)

**Success Criteria:**
- Metrics visible at a glance
- Can drill into details easily
- Actionable insights (not just data)

---

### Workflow 5: Update Business Information

**Frequency:** Quarterly or as needed  
**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Steps:**
1. Go to Business Settings
2. Update relevant fields:
   - Address (if moved)
   - Phone (if changed)
   - Insurance (if renewed)
   - Billing info
3. Save changes
4. System notifies connected banks (if relevant)

**Success Criteria:**
- Easy to find and update
- Clear what's required vs optional
- Changes propagate appropriately

## Edge Cases & Special Scenarios

### 1. Admin Who Is Not an Appraiser
**Example:** Robert Taylor (Operations Manager at Coastal Appraisal Group)
- Has user account (Admin role)
- Does NOT have appraiser profile
- Can manage team, view work, handle admin
- Cannot do appraisal work

**Solution:**
- Onboarding asks: "Are you also an appraiser?" → NO
- Skips appraiser profile creation
- Full admin access without personal profile

---

### 2. Appraiser Who Leaves Company
**Scenario:** Maria quits, joins competitor

**Actions:**
1. Sarah deactivates Maria's user account
2. Maria's appraiser profile stays (historical data)
3. Maria's active work reassigned to others
4. Maria's profile marked "Inactive"
5. Banks notified Maria no longer with company
6. Maria cannot log in anymore

**Solution:**
- Deactivate vs Delete distinction
- Historical data preserved
- Clean offboarding process

---

### 3. Solo Appraiser Who Grows Into Business
**Scenario:** Started as individual, now hiring first employee

**Actions:**
1. Contact support: "I need to upgrade to business"
2. Admin converts account to business entity
3. Original profile becomes "business" with one appraiser
4. Can now invite team members
5. Gets business features

**Solution:**
- Account upgrade path
- Data migration from individual → business
- No loss of existing work/connections

---
